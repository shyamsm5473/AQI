Project Impact

Core Benefits & Strategic Value

Discover how this digital monitoring platform bridges environmental engineering and community data access to provide tangible results across Uttar Pradesh.

🫁

Public Health Protection

Empowering the local community with explicit safety benchmarks:

  • Color-Coded Tiers: Translates abstract pollutant numbers into clear, actionable CPCB health risk classifications.
  • Minimized Exposure: Allows students, staff, and citizens to safely time outdoor exercises around ideal air windows.
  • Vulnerable Group Alerts: Offers baseline visual insight warning high-risk respiratory patients before peak haze hours.
Fostering informed environmental lifestyle choices.
📈

Granular Pollutant Analysis

Comprehensive tracing of fine, microscopic, and gaseous parameters:

  • Dual Particle Tracking: Monitors respirable PM2.5 and coarse PM10 inhalable particles concurrently.
  • Combustion Indicators: Tracks industrial and traffic effluents including Carbon Monoxide (CO) and Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂).
  • Meteorological Interactivity: Maps temperature and humidity markers alongside active AQI values.
Extracting full value from Open-Meteo atmospheric models[cite: 2].
💻

Scalable Digital Platform

A smart web architecture minimizing maintenance barriers:

  • Hardware Independence: Relies on cloud APIs rather than expensive local sensor arrays.
  • Zero Cost Expansion: Can instantly onboard additional city zones without installation logistics.
  • Excel Exporting: Download full granular sets across selected historical tracking blocks with one click.
Designed for extreme cost-effectiveness and durability.
🗺️

State-Wide GIS Tracking

Clear regional telemetry through customized interactive map models:

  • District Level Clusters: Dynamically visualizes data across all 75 Uttar Pradesh administrative locations.
  • Location Auto-Detection: Supports native browser geolocation to quickly pin users to the closest monitoring spot.
  • Smart Aggregation: Automatically bundles nearby stations while highlighting the worst localized reading inside.
Supported by UPCST to encourage open data outreach[cite: 2, 5].
