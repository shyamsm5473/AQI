About this project

Real-Time Air Quality Monitoring for Lucknow

This platform tracks live air quality and weather conditions across Lucknow — including the IIIT Lucknow campus, Hazratganj, Gomti Nagar, and Alambagh — through an interactive AQI gauge, 24-hour pollutant trends, and a station-by-station readings table, so students, staff, and the public can check the air they're breathing in real time.

🏛️

Who Made It

This project is a joint initiative between the Indian Institute of Information Technology, Lucknow (IIITL) and the Uttar Pradesh Council of Science & Technology (UPCST). IIITL led the research, design, and technical development, with UPCST supporting the initiative as part of its mandate to advance science and technology outreach across Uttar Pradesh.

📡

Where the Data Comes From

The dashboard pulls live readings directly from the Open-Meteo Air Quality & Weather APIs, which combine ground-monitoring stations with atmospheric models to estimate:

  • PM2.5 & PM10 (particulate matter)
  • Carbon Monoxide (CO) & Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂)
  • Ozone, temperature & humidity
This keeps the system free to run and easy to extend to new locations without installing hardware.

Synthesis of Big Data using Federated Learning for Analyzing Climate and Pollution Parameters in Edge Networks for Urban Areas

A privacy-preserving edge intelligence framework: models travel between sensors and the cloud, so raw climate and pollution data never has to.

Funding Agency Council of Science & Technology (CST), Uttar Pradesh
Host Institute Dept. of Computer Science, IIIT Lucknow
Location Chak Ganjaria, C.G. City, Lucknow – 226002
01

Project Summary

Urban environments generate enormous volumes of climate and air-pollution data through heterogeneous IoT sensors. Conventional cloud-based analytics require continuous transmission of raw sensor data to centralized servers, driving up communication overhead, latency, bandwidth use, and privacy risk.

This project develops a privacy-preserving Federated Learning (FL) framework for synthesizing and analyzing climate and pollution big data. Rather than transferring raw data, machine learning models are trained collaboratively across geographically distributed edge devices, with data staying where it's collected.

The framework brings together Federated Learning, Edge Computing, Big Data Analytics, Artificial Intelligence, and IoT into a scalable, real-time, communication-efficient environmental monitoring system for smart city applications — including novel federated optimization algorithms, intelligent data fusion, adaptive model aggregation, and edge-cloud orchestration.

  • Develop privacy-preserving federated learning models for climate and pollution analytics.
  • Design communication-efficient model aggregation techniques for distributed edge networks.
  • Develop intelligent data fusion techniques for heterogeneous environmental datasets.

  • Optimize latency, communication cost, and energy consumption in edge intelligence.
  • Develop deployable prototypes for smart city environmental monitoring.
  • Enable accurate prediction of air quality and climate parameters using distributed AI.
