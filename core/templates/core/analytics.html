Air Quality Findings

224 AQI readings collected between Dec 30, 2025 and Mar 30, 2026 — explored below through category breakdown, peak vs. baseline trends, spike events, weekly exposure, and temperature correlation.

Temperature vs. AQI

Pearson r = -0.58 — AQI tends to fall as temperature rises (winter haze vs. spring dispersion).

Scatter of temperature against AQI: cooler winter readings around 14 to 18 degrees cluster at higher AQI values up to 315, while warmer March readings around 28 to 30 degrees cluster at lower AQI values mostly under 200.

Peak vs. Baseline AQI Over Time

Peak and baseline AQI tracked closely from Dec 30, 2025 to Mar 30, 2026, alongside a 3-day rolling exposure trend.

Peak AQI Baseline AQI 3-day exposure trend
Peak AQI ranges from 53 to 315.7 across the dataset, generally tracking baseline AQI closely, with higher values in January and lower values in late February and March.

Short-Term Spike & Drop Events

30 events sorted by deviation from the local rolling mean — largest jump was +65.2 AQI on Jan 30, largest drop was -65.2 AQI on Feb 3.
